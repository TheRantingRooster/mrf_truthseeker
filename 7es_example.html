1 Input: The Gateway
Input is everything that enters your system from the outside world. Think of it as the raw material that gets your system moving - like fuel in a car, food in your body, or data in a computer.
🌟 Real-World Examples
Your Brain
Visual signals, sounds, thoughts, emotions entering your consciousness
Coffee Shop
Customers, coffee beans, water, electricity, staff arriving
Solar System
Cosmic radiation, asteroids, comets entering from interstellar space
2 Output: The Purpose
Output is what your system creates and sends back to the world. It's the reason the system exists - the value it adds, the change it makes, the energy it releases.
🎯 Real-World Examples
Your Brain
Decisions, movements, speech, creative ideas, memories
Coffee Shop
Hot coffee, satisfied customers, revenue, community gathering space
Solar System
Planetary motion, heat/light radiation, gravitational influence on nearby stars
3 Processing: The Magic
Processing is where the transformation happens - the mysterious "black box" where inputs become outputs. It's metabolism in your body, thinking in your brain, brewing in a coffee shop.
⚡ Real-World Examples
Your Brain
Neural firing, pattern recognition, memory formation, emotional processing
Coffee Shop
Grinding beans, brewing coffee, taking orders, creating atmosphere
Solar System
Gravitational interactions, orbital mechanics, energy transfer between bodies
4 Controls: The Rules
Controls are the built-in rules and constraints that keep your system working properly. They're like the guardrails that prevent chaos - DNA in cells, laws in society, physics in the universe.
🎛️ Real-World Examples
Your Brain
Neural pathways, cognitive biases, instinctive responses, learned habits
Coffee Shop
Recipes, health codes, business hours, pricing policies, staff procedures
Solar System
Laws of physics, gravitational constants, conservation of energy
5 Feedback: The Learning Loop
Feedback is how your system learns and adapts. It's the information that comes back from your outputs, telling you whether you're on track or need to adjust course.
🔄 Real-World Examples
Your Brain
Pain signals, satisfaction, success/failure experiences, social reactions
Coffee Shop
Customer reviews, sales data, repeat customers, staff observations
Solar System
Gravitational perturbations, orbital adjustments, tidal effects
6 Interface: The Boundary
Interface is where your system meets the world - the boundary that defines what's inside vs outside, and how exchanges happen. It's your skin, a website's user interface, or a cell membrane.
🌐 Real-World Examples
Your Brain
Sensory organs, nervous system, speech/language, facial expressions
Coffee Shop
Front counter, menu board, door, website, social media presence
Solar System
Heliopause, magnetic field boundaries, gravitational influence zones
7 Environment: The Context
Environment is everything outside your system that can influence it. It provides the context, opportunities, threats, and resources that shape how your system behaves and evolves.
🌍 Real-World Examples
Your Brain
Cultural context, social relationships, physical health, life experiences
Coffee Shop
Local economy, competition, weather, suppliers, neighborhood culture
Solar System
Milky Way galaxy, nearby star systems, interstellar medium, cosmic forces
